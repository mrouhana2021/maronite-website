WHO WE ARE

A Community of
Faith & Heritage

The Maronite Association of British Columbia unites Maronite Catholics in the Greater Vancouver area, preserving our ancient Syriac Christian heritage while building a vibrant community in the heart of Canada.

"Rooted in the faith of Saint Maron, we gather as one family — in prayer, in culture, and in love for God."

We are part of the Eparchy of Saint Maron of Canada and serve Maronite families throughout BC with liturgical celebrations, community gatherings, cultural events, and charitable works. All are welcome.

Our community worships at École Saint-Sacrament — 3020 Heather Street, Vancouver, where we gather for the Holy Qurbono (Divine Liturgy), the Rosary, and fellowship.

OUR TRADITION

Rooted in 1,600 Years
of Living Faith

✝

The Holy Qurbono

The Maronite Divine Liturgy traces its roots to the ancient Antiochene Syriac rite. Celebrated in Aramaic, Arabic, and English, it connects us to the earliest Christians of the Levant.

📿

The Holy Rosary

We gather in prayer before every community celebration to pray the Rosary together, honoring Our Lady of Lebanon — patroness of the Maronite people and of peace.

✦

The Cedar of Lebanon

The Cedar is our eternal symbol — standing tall through centuries, rooted deep. Like the cedars of Lebanon praised in Scripture, our faith endures through every storm.

☪

Saint Maron

Our patron Saint Maron (350–410 AD) was a Syrian monk whose holiness inspired thousands. His disciples spread the faith through Mount Lebanon, founding our great Church.

✝

Full Communion

The Maronite Church is an Eastern Catholic Church in full and continuous communion with the Pope in Rome — never broken throughout our 1,600-year history.

🏔

Lebanese Heritage

We celebrate the rich culture of Lebanon — its music, cuisine, language, and spirit of resilience — as an integral part of who we are as a community in Vancouver.
